The Genealogy Day at Tampere's Sampola community centre on Saturday 28 January went well. After that I travelled to Helsinki for the Finnish Genealogical Society's 100th anniversary celebration at the House of Estates (*Säätytalo*) on 30 January — exactly 100 years after the society's founding meeting.

The ceremony was impressive. A minister, a bishop and a director general all gave speeches; the soprano Aliina Koivula provided the music. Matti has been a member of the Finnish Genealogical Society since the late 1960s and served on its board for two decades, so the centenary felt like a personal milestone as well.

It was a good week for genealogy in Finland.
